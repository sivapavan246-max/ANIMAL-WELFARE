THE ANIMAL ALLIANCE

COW/BUFFALO

Cows and buffaloes are susceptible to various infectious, parasitic, and metabolic diseases. Treatment and prevention often involve hygiene, isolation of sick animals, and vaccination, but a veterinarian should always be consulted for specific diagnoses and treatment plans.

Common diseases and treatments

DISEASE REASON SYMPTOMS TREATMENT
*Anthrax Bacterial Sudden death, high fever, difficulty breathing, bloody discharge from natural openings. Prompt administration of antibiotics (penicillin, tetracycline). Prevention through vaccination and proper disposal (burning) of carcasses.
*Black Quarter (Blackleg) Bacterial Fever, lameness, crepitating (crackling) swelling in the hind or forequarters due to gas accumulation in the muscle. Penicillin or tetracycline, especially if administered early and into the lesion site. Vaccination is a key preventive measure.
*Milk Fever (Hypocalcemia) Metabolic Occurs typically within 48 hours of calving; loss of appetite, constipation, restlessness, muscle tremors, inability to stand (recumbency). Immediate intravenous administration of calcium gluconate solution by a veterinarian, followed by oral calcium boluses. Prevention involves dietary management (low calcium diet beforecalving) and calcium supplements.

General Management and Prevention

Hygiene: Maintain clean living areas, especially around water troughs and milking parlors, to minimize the spread of soil-borne or bacterial infections.
Vaccination: Follow a regular vaccination schedule for common diseases like Black Quarter and FMD, consulting with a local veterinarian for the appropriate vaccines and timing.
Isolation: Isolate sick animals immediately to prevent the spread of contagious diseases to the rest of the herd.
Nutrition: Ensure a balanced diet with adequate minerals and vitamins to maximize hoof health and boost immunity.
Vector Control: Manage ticks, flies, and mosquitoes, as they can transmit diseases like Bluetongue, Anaplasmosis, and Babesiosis.
Professional Consultation: For any serious or unusual signs of disease, contact a qualified veterinarian immediately.
