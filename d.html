THE ANIMAL ALLIANCE

DOGS

Common dog diseases include viral infections like parvovirus and distemper, parasitic infections such as heartworms and intestinal worms, and other conditions like kennel cough, ear infections, and allergies. Prevention through vaccination, regular vet check-ups, and proper nutrition is crucial. Treatment varies by disease, ranging from supportive care and medication for viral infections to preventative medication for parasites.

Common diseases and treatments

DISEASE SYMPTOMS TREATMENT
Canine Parvovirus (Parvo): Severe diarrhea (often bloody), vomiting, lethargy, loss of appetite, dehydration, fever. Intensive supportive care, including IV fluids, electrolyte balance, and medication to control vomiting and diarrhea. No specific cure for the virus itself.
Rabies: Behavioral changes (aggression/sudden calmness), excessive salivation, difficulty swallowing, paralysis. No cure once symptoms appear; it is a fatal viral disease. Prevention via vaccination is critical and legally required in many areas.
Ear Infections (Otitis): Redness, discharge, foul odor from the ear canal, head shaking, scratching at ears. Thorough cleaning by a vet, prescribed ear drops or oral medication (antibiotics, antifungals, anti-inflammatories).
Canine Distemper: Coughing, sneezing, nasal/eye discharge, fever, lethargy, neurological signs (twitching, seizures). Supportive care to manage symptoms; no specific cure. Often fatal.
Kennel Cough: Persistent, forceful hacking cough, sneezing, nasal discharge. Most dogs recover in 3-6 weeks; treatment may include cough suppressants and antibiotics (if bacterial infection present).
Dental Disease: Bad breath, yellow/brown tartar on teeth, red/bleeding gums, difficulty eating, loose teeth. Professional dental cleaning under anesthesia, potential tooth extraction, and at-home dental hygiene (brushing, special diets).
Arthritis: Lameness, stiff gait, difficulty getting up, swollen joints, reluctance to jump or play. Management with anti-inflammatory medications, joint supplements, weight management, and physical therapy; no cure.
