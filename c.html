THE ANIMAL ALLIANCE

CATS

Common cat diseases include upper respiratory infections, flea and parasite infestations, dental disease, obesity, and chronic conditions like kidney disease and diabetes. Treatment varies from parasite prevention and antibiotics to managing diet and providing supportive care, but a veterinarian must diagnose and treat most illnesses. Key prevention methods include regular vet visits, annual vaccinations, parasite prevention, and keeping indoor cats indoors.

Common diseases and treatments

DISEASE SYMPTOMS TREATMENT
Upper Respiratory Infections (URI) (Cat Flu): Sneezing, coughing, nasal discharge, eye discharge Antibiotics (if bacterial), antiviral medications, eye drops, supportive care like keeping the cat comfortable.
Fleas & Parasites: Excessive scratching, flea dirt in fur, skin irritation, vomiting (for worms) Monthly parasite preventatives, environmental control (keeping living spaces clean), vet-prescribed deworming medication
Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease (FLUTD): Difficulty urinating, straining, frequent urination in small amounts, blood in urine Vet visit is critical for accurate diagnosis. Treatment may involve medication, stress reduction, and dietary changes
FeLV & FIV: FeLV: weakened immune system, cancer. FIV: similar to human HIV, a weakened immune system FeLV: No cure, but management includes preventing secondary infections and supportive care. FIV: No cure, but management includes supportive care, good nutrition, and keeping the cat indoors and away from other cats.

General Management and Prevention

Prevention is key

*Vaccinate: Keep your cat's vaccinations up to date to prevent serious diseases like FeLV and rabies.
*Annual vet visits: Regular check-ups help catch problems early, especially in senior cats.
*Parasite control: Use regular preventative treatments to protect against fleas, ticks, and worms.
*Maintain a clean environment: Keep your cat's living area, food, and water bowls clean.
*Indoor lifestyle: Keeping cats indoors can significantly reduce their risk of exposure to contagious diseases.
